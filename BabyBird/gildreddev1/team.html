George Gildred

Director of Acquisitions and Dispositions

George has provided leadership and guidance at the Gildred Companies for over fifty years. Active in real estate investment in the area for many decades, George is committed to his community. He was recognized as one of San Diego’s distinguished leaders when he received the Mr. San Diego Award, an award which his father, Philip Gildred Sr. received in 1974 for service to the community. He served as Chair of the San Diego Convention and Visitors Bureau, Hubbs Sea World Research Institute, the University of California San Diego Development Council and the Committee to build the University of California San Diego Cancer Center.

George is past Chairman and President of San Diego Zoo Global and he led the first Zoo delegation to China in 1979. He has also provided leadership in the community as the Co-Chairman of the Citizens Committee to build the San Diego Stadium (formerly Qualcomm Stadium) and is a Past President of the Rotary Club of San Diego. He serves as Honorary Consul of the Republic of Chile in San Diego. Following graduation from Stanford University, George served as an officer in the United States Navy from 1956 to 1960.

Phil Gildred

Director of Acquisitions and Dispositions

Phil Gildred is the Gildred Companies’ most tenured employee providing leadership to the company since 1961. The Gildred Companies has a legacy of developing commercial real estate throughout the San Diego community, and one of giving back in the process. Phil shares his father’s vision of making a difference and has always been involved in San Diego business and philanthropy.

He is the Founding President of both the Sharp Hospital Foundation and the University Club Atop Symphony Towers. Phil is the past President of the Board of several San Diego organizations including the San Diego Chapter of Building Owners and Managers Association, Chairman of the Pacific (Southwest Regional Conference), the Executives Association of San Diego, Board of Directors Francis W. Parker School and the San Diego Automotive Museum.

He has a long list of philanthropic and civic organizations on whose boards he has served including the American Red Cross and the YMCA Camp Committee. He is a long term Board Member of the San Diego Historical Society and served as its Vice President.

Rene LeBlanc

Chief Financial Officer

Rene LeBlanc is the Chief Financial Officer of the Gildred Companies and has more than 33 years of real estate tax and accounting experience. A Certified Public Accountant, she has been a member of the team since 1994.

Before her tenure with Gildred, she worked for the CPA firm Lavine, Lofgren, Morris & Engelberg, LLP, where Gildred was a client. Prior to that she gained experience as a Tax Manager with the National accounting firm Kenneth Leventhal & Company which specializes in real estate. Rene is excited to bring her expertise in real estate tax and financial planning to the team. She is responsible for financial planning and forecasting, strategic tax planning, financial accounting and analysis, arranging financing, treasury and key administrative functions.

Enthusiastic about children’s causes, Rene volunteers and serves on the boards of educational institutions and sports organizations in our community. She is a third generation native of La Jolla and loves traveling, hiking and exploring the outdoors.

Gregg Haggart

CEO

Gregg Haggart feels fortunate to lead a team that is committed to continuing the Gildred family’s legacy of positively impacting San Diego through the company’s business operations. He joined the Gildred team in 2006.

Gregg has more than 30 years of real estate, finance, energy efficiency and renewable energy experience with industry leading companies including Sempra Energy, Honeywell and ABB. Aside from implementing best practices in the development and operation of Gildred’s real estate portfolio, with respect to energy/water efficiency and sustainability, the company is developing a 50 megawatt solar project in the eastern part of San Diego County to provide clean renewable power to 17,000 homes.

A passionate advocate for students, Gregg has volunteered and presented in classrooms for decades, is founding member and Treasurer of the Borrego Springs Education Foundation and is Chairman of the Board of Audeo II Charter School. Both of these organizations directly impact the lives of students.

For fun Gregg loves traveling, especially adventure touring with his wife on their motorcycle. He has also raced his dirt bike in the Baja 250, 500 (solo) and 1000 events always finishing, sometimes on the podium.
